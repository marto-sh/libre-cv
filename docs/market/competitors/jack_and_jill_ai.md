# Jack & Jill AI

> **Website:** https://jackandjill.ai/
> **Founded:** Early 2025, London
> **Founders:** Matthew Wilson (prev. Omnipresent), Saaras Mehan (prev. Kular.ai, YC-backed)

## Company Overview

Jack & Jill is a conversational AI recruitment platform that places autonomous agents on both sides of the hiring process. The company raised a **$20M seed round** (Oct 2025) led by Creandum (early Spotify/Klarna backer), with 75+ angel investors from Anthropic, Lovable, and ElevenLabs. Currently live in London (~50,000 users), with U.S. expansion planned (San Francisco).

The vision: replace the traditional recruiter with AI agents that deliver "the service of a world-class recruiter at a fraction of the cost."

## Product/Service Offering

Two conversational AI agents capable of thousands of simultaneous conversations:

- **Jack (job seekers):** Conducts a 20-minute conversational intake to deeply understand skills, experience, and career goals. Then proactively searches 10,000+ new jobs hourly to curate matches. Also provides salary benchmarking, CV/LinkedIn optimization, interview prep, and negotiation coaching.
- **Jill (employers):** Takes a hiring brief, builds a role profile, sources candidates from Jack's network, and delivers vetted introductions — often same-day.

**USP:** Dialogue-based discovery that captures nuance traditional job boards miss. Candidates describe it as "a career coach" rather than an automated system.

## Pricing Strategy

- **Job seekers:** Free.
- **Employers:** 10% of first-year salary on successful placement, with a 3-month money-back guarantee. Positioned as half the cost of traditional agency recruiters (~20%).

## Marketing & Messaging

- **Target audience:** Tech professionals seeking roles at well-funded startups; growth-stage companies needing efficient hiring.
- **Key messages:**
  - "Polite, not pushy, and genuinely helpful" — contrast with aggressive recruiters.
  - Conversational AI that understands your "messy, non-linear experience in a way job boards never could."
  - Speed and personalization that traditional processes can't match.

## Market Perception

**Trustpilot: 4.0/5** (129 reviews)

### Strengths

- Strong personalization — users feel the AI genuinely understands their profile and career aspirations.
- Time-saving — Jack surfaces opportunities while candidates focus on what matters.
- Intuitive onboarding and user-friendly interface.
- Fast for employers — vetted candidates delivered same-day.
- Significant early traction (49,000 candidates, hundreds of companies, notable client TrueLayer).

### Weaknesses

- **Technical glitches:** Reports of Jack entering repetitive conversational loops; audio issues on intake calls.
- **Support responsiveness:** Multiple reports of unanswered support requests.
- **Job listing accuracy:** Some outdated postings and occasional mismatched recommendations.
- **Dashboard limitations:** No built-in application tracking — users resort to external spreadsheets.
- **Industry criticism:** Treats recruitment as a matching problem, but hiring requires trust-building and contextual judgment that AI cannot replicate. Data cited: 78% of job seekers prefer human interaction; 40% higher first-year attrition with fully automated systems.

## Relevance to libre-cv

Jack & Jill is the closest competitor in spirit — an AI agent that represents you professionally. Key differences:

| Dimension | Jack & Jill | libre-cv |
|---|---|---|
| Data ownership | Platform owns your data | You own your data |
| Code | Proprietary | Libre / open source |
| Business model | Commission-based (you're the product) | Self-hosted (you're the owner) |
| Agent scope | Job search only | General professional representation |
| Identity | Locked in their platform | Portable, structured, queryable |

Jack & Jill validates the core premise that professionals want an agent acting on their behalf. Its limitations — proprietary lock-in, commission model, narrow job-search focus — are precisely the gaps libre-cv aims to fill.
