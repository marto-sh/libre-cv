# Maayu

> **Website:** https://www.maayu.ai/
> **Founded:** 2024, Wilmington, DE (founders based in Ashburn, VA)
> **Founders:** Prabakaran Murugaiah (CEO, serial entrepreneur), Deepa Sankaralingam (COO)

## Company Overview

Maayu is an early-stage, **unfunded** startup building "autonomous digital workforce" agents, starting with recruitment. The team is small (likely under 10 people). Won best startup idea at EMERGE 2024 (TNF conference, Chicago). Ranked 135th out of 728 active competitors on Tracxn.

The vision extends beyond recruitment: "autonomously handle any business process across any industry." Currently operates in the US (maayu.ai) and India (maayuai.in).

## Product/Service Offering

Creates individual trainable AI assistants called "maayus" for three user types:

**For Candidates:**
- Profile optimization and job matching
- Assisted apply with step-by-step guidance
- Interview simulator with role-specific questions
- Salary negotiation assistant

**For Recruiters:**
- Pre-screening, validation, and verification of candidates
- Autonomous sourcing-to-placement pipeline
- ATS integration (Greenhouse, JobDiva, Bullhorn, CEIPAL)

**For Hiring Managers:**
- Candidate shortlisting and interview scheduling

**USP:** Individual "maayus" (digital twins) per person with self-guided learning. Multi-modal capabilities (voice, expressions, posture analysis). Claims to clone itself for parallel task handling.

**Unvalidated performance claims:**
- 65% reduction in hiring costs
- 50% reduction in time-to-fill

## Pricing Strategy

**No public pricing.** Sales-led with demo requests for the employer side. Likely free or freemium for candidates. Consistent with an early-stage startup pricing through direct negotiation.

### Strengths (based on positioning, not validated)

- "Digital twin" per person is conceptually differentiated.
- Dual-sided platform (candidates + recruiters) has network effects potential.
- Active content marketing despite small team.
- India presence gives access to a large talent market.

### Weaknesses

- No funding, no disclosed revenue, no disclosed user numbers.
- No independent reviews or social proof anywhere.
- Performance claims appear unsubstantiated.
- Very small team for an ambitious "autonomous digital workforce" vision.
- Aggressive "autonomous" claims for current state of AI agents.
- Virtual office registration (common for small startups, but not confidence-inspiring).

## Relevance to libre-cv

Maayu is the most conceptually adjacent competitor — it explicitly uses the "digital twin" framing with individual AI agents per person. However, the philosophies diverge sharply:

| Dimension | Maayu | libre-cv |
|---|---|---|
| Data ownership | Platform owns your data | You own your data |
| Code | Proprietary | Libre / open source |
| Digital twin scope | Recruitment-focused | General professional identity |
| Business model | Proprietary SaaS (likely) | Self-hosted |
| Portability | Locked in platform | Portable, structured, queryable |
| Maturity | Pre-traction, unfunded | — |

Maayu validates that the "individual digital twin" concept has market appeal. Not a significant competitive threat in its current state, but worth monitoring if it secures funding.
