# Jobright

> **Website:** https://jobright.ai/
> **Founded:** 2023, Santa Clara, CA
> **Founders:** Eric Yuan Cheng, Ethan Yudian Zheng (AI veterans from Google, Twitter, Box, TikTok)

## Company Overview

Jobright is an AI-powered job search platform that evolved from a matching tool into an autonomous agent ("Jobright Agent") that searches, customizes, and submits applications on behalf of candidates. The company raised **$7.7M total** (latest: $3.2M seed in June 2025, led by Translink Capital with participation from Indeed's venture arm). Claims **520,000+ users** and **$1M revenue** (Sep 2025), with a team of ~78 people.

Positioned as "the first AI agent to put job search on autopilot."

## Product/Service Offering

- **AI Job Matching:** Scans thousands of sources and evaluates mutual fit between candidate and role.
- **Jobright Agent:** Autonomously finds, customizes, and submits applications without manual intervention.
- **Resume Tailoring:** AI-generated resumes optimized for specific roles and ATS systems.
- **Autofill Chrome Extension:** Fills ATS application forms automatically (100,000+ users, 4.6/5 rating).
- **AI Assistant "Orion":** Career guidance, interview prep, and coaching.
- **Insider Connections:** Surfaces networking opportunities and referral paths.
- **H1B Job Listings:** Dedicated filter for visa-sponsored positions.

**USP:** End-to-end automation — from discovery to application submission. Claims 2x more interviews and 80% less time spent on job search.

## Pricing Strategy

**Opaque.** No public pricing table. The platform leads with "Try Free" but exact costs are only visible at checkout and reportedly change frequently. The free tier provides meaningful access to core tools; paid upgrades likely add volume limits or advanced features.

This lack of transparency is a recurring user complaint.

## Marketing & Messaging

- **Target audience:** U.S.-based job seekers, high-volume applicants.
- **Key messages:**
  - "For decades, job boards made you work. Now, our agent works for you."
  - Emphasis on autopilot, time savings, and doubling interview rates.
  - 520,000+ users as social proof.
- **Geographic limitation:** U.S. only — explicitly excludes international job seekers.

## Market Perception

**Trustpilot: 2.9/5** — mixed sentiment.

### Strengths

- Chrome extension autofill is the "sticky daily-use component" — genuinely useful for high-volume applicants.
- Real adoption at scale (520k users, 100k extension installs).
- Addresses a well-documented pain point (application fatigue).
- Free tier provides real value for initial exploration.

### Weaknesses

- **Job listing quality:** Fake postings, scams, duplicates, and expired positions surfaced by the system.
- **AI output quality:** Resumes have formatting issues, inconsistent spacing, and generic content. Hallucinations require manual supervision.
- **U.S. only:** No international coverage.
- **Pricing opacity:** Frequent changes, no upfront transparency — erodes trust.
- **Support issues:** Multiple reports of unresponsive customer support.
- **Privacy concerns:** Extension reads sensitive ATS data — problematic for regulated industries.
- **Auto-apply reportedly still in beta** — core promise not fully delivered.

## Relevance to libre-cv

| Dimension | Jobright | libre-cv |
|---|---|---|
| Data ownership | Platform owns your data | You own your data |
| Code | Proprietary | Libre / open source |
| Business model | Freemium SaaS, opaque pricing | Self-hosted |
| Agent scope | Job application automation | General professional representation |
| Geography | U.S. only | No geographic restriction |
| Identity | Profile locked in platform | Portable, structured, queryable |
| Privacy | Extension reads ATS data | You control all data flows |

Jobright validates that job seekers want automation and an agent working on their behalf. However, its approach — bulk-applying with questionable quality, opaque pricing, U.S.-only, and proprietary data — represents the opposite of libre-cv's philosophy. Its poor Trustpilot score (2.9/5) and quality issues suggest that rushing to automate without a strong identity foundation leads to low-quality outcomes.
